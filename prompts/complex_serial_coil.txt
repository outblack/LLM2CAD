Create one continuous, unbranched hollow pipe following a single serial centerline. Do not add junctions, caps, plugs, valves, or disconnected bodies.

Begin at the origin with the centerline traveling in the +X direction. Use a nominal outer diameter of 18 mm and a wall thickness of 2 mm through the main body.

First create a straight inlet run about 55 mm long. The inlet at the origin must remain physically open, with its inner bore and annular wall rim visible.

After the straight run, turn the pipe broadly upward through approximately 90 degrees using a centerline bend radius of about 45 mm. The bend should finish generally in the +Z direction and must look like a broad smooth elbow, not a tight hook or a polygonal arc.

From the upper end of that bend, ease into a rising three-dimensional coil. This must be a spatial spline-like route rather than a flat circular ring. Use the following ordered waypoint offsets relative to the coil inlet, expressed in global XYZ axes, in millimeters:
   (0, 5, 20),
   (0, 20, 35),
   (0, 40, 40),
   (35, 90, 45),
   (70, 100, 50),
   (110, 80, 55),
   (130, 40, 60),
   (110, 0, 65),
   (70, -15, 70),
   (35, 0, 75),
   (20, 40, 80),
   (35, 75, 80),
   (70, 90, 85).
These points include a broad entry easing stage and then travel about one and one-quarter turns around an open central region. The route rises by 85 mm overall. Its plan view must read as the deliberately nonuniform tightening coil encoded by those waypoints, not as a perfect circular helix. Preserve ample pitch and clearance so neighboring turns and nonadjacent pipe regions do not touch.

After the coil, create a separate three-dimensional S-shaped spline stage using these ordered global-axis offsets relative to that stage's own inlet, in millimeters:
   (35, 15, 5),
   (70, 50, 10),
   (110, 70, 20),
   (150, 30, 25),
   (190, -20, 35).
The first offset continues the coil's final heading. The path then departs laterally, reverses the lateral direction to form a second broad lobe, and rises by 35 mm overall. The two lobes should be clearly distinguishable in more than one orthographic view. They must not collapse into a nearly straight segment, a flat planar S, or a pair of sharp elbows.

Next create another distinct freeform spline stage using these ordered global-axis offsets relative to the escape-stage inlet, in millimeters:
   (40, -50, 10),
   (70, -70, 10),
   (100, -60, 15),
   (130, -30, 20),
   (160, 0, 25),
   (200, 0, 25).
This stage opens away from the coil's central region, rises slightly, and its final chord settles exactly toward the horizontal +X outlet direction. It must not curl back toward or cross the coil.

Only after the route is nearly straight, add a coaxial tapered outlet approximately 45 mm long. Reduce the outer diameter smoothly from 18 mm to 12 mm and the wall thickness from 2 mm to 1.5 mm. The taper must be gradual, with no abrupt shoulder, and the final outlet must remain open.

Keep a circular hollow cross-section and continuous unobstructed bore throughout. The finished result must be one valid connected pipe body with exactly one downstream outlet in addition to the anchored open inlet. Avoid self-intersection, pinching, blocked bore regions, coincident waypoints, or nonadjacent surface contact.

Every stage boundary must have aligned centerline tangents and a fair visual transition. Exact mathematical curvature equality at every boundary is not required, but there must be no visible kink, sudden curvature reversal, faceted bend, ribbed surface, or abrupt diameter change. Use smooth spline geometry for the coil, S-stage, and outward escape stage, and keep their roles visually distinct.
