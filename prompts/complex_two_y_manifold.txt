Create one connected hollow circular-pipe manifold in millimeters with four physical open ends in total. Anchor START at global position (0, 0, 0), with the pipe entering the generated geometry along +X. START is one of the four physical openings, but it is the fixed upstream interface and must not be counted as a generated downstream outlet. Use 20 mm outside diameter and 2 mm wall thickness through the inlet, the first junction, its terminal side branch, and the central route until the later taper.

1. Begin with a positive 70 mm straight inlet run along +X.

2. At the end of that straight, create the first smooth binary Y junction. It must have exactly two outputs: one internal primary continuation for the later central route, and one final terminal branch. Preserve the primary continuation approximately along the incoming +X direction. Make the final branch a long spatial diagonal arm with global axis proportional to (+1, -1, +1), centerline length 85 mm, outside diameter 20 mm, and wall thickness 2 mm. Leave that branch end open permanently. Use a genuinely blended hub rather than hard intersecting cylinders: outer blend radius 5 mm, inner-bore blend radius 3 mm, and maximum hub radius 25 mm. Do not count the primary continuation as a final terminal at this junction.

3. From the first Y's primary continuation--not from its final diagonal branch--build one smooth, nonplanar central spline route. Its waypoint offsets are relative to that selected primary outlet, expressed in global XYZ axes, and must be visited in this order:
   (40, 0, 0),
   (75, 30, 25),
   (110, 55, 55),
   (150, 20, 80),
   (190, 0, 100) mm.
The first offset is a +X lead-in compatible with the primary outlet. The later offsets make a broad rising spatial S-like path that moves toward +Y, then returns toward Y=0 while continuing upward. Use smooth tangent-continuous bends, no sharp waypoint corners, no tight loop, and no self-intersection. The outlet heading of this spline is intentionally established by its last chord, proportional to (+2, -1, +1).

4. Continue straight along that spline outlet heading with one concentric 45 mm long gradual taper. Reduce the outside diameter smoothly from 20 mm to 16 mm and the wall thickness from 2 mm to 1.5 mm. Keep both the outer surface and bore transition gradual and unobstructed; do not use a step change or eccentric offset.

5. At the taper outlet, create the second smooth binary Y junction. This junction must have exactly two final terminal branches and no primary or straight-through third outlet. Make its first final arm point along a global axis proportional to (+1, +1, +1), with 95 mm centerline length, 16 mm outside diameter, and 1.5 mm wall thickness. Make its second final arm point along a global axis proportional to (+1, -1, -1), with 115 mm centerline length, 16 mm outside diameter, and 1.5 mm wall thickness. Leave both ends open. Blend this smaller Y with outer radius 4 mm, inner-bore radius 2.5 mm, and maximum hub radius 20 mm.

The final topology must therefore be: START -> straight inlet -> first binary Y; one first-Y branch terminates open, while the first-Y primary continues through the 3D spline and taper -> second binary Y -> two open final branches. There must be exactly 3 free generated downstream open ports, which together with START give exactly 4 physical open ends. Do not add any caps, plugs, extra open-end marker, connector, third branch, or duplicate START arm.

All geometry must form one connected material body. The internal flow space must be one continuous bifurcated hollow network from START to all three downstream openings, with positive wall thickness everywhere. Avoid arm-to-arm collision, retracing toward the inlet, non-adjacent overlap, blocked or pinched bores, disconnected solids, zero-thickness regions, and self-intersection. Keep all three terminal arms and the rising central route visually distinguishable in isometric, top, front, and side views.
