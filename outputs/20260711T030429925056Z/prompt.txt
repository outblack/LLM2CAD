Create a single continuous hollow pipe with a serpentine multi-bend shape.

The pipe should be one connected body with a uniform circular cross-section. Use an outer diameter of approximately 20 mm and a wall thickness of approximately 2 mm.

The overall shape should follow this sequence:

1. Start with a vertical pipe rising upward on the left side, with an open top end. The left vertical section should be approximately 130 mm tall.
2. From the bottom of this left vertical pipe, create a smooth large U-shaped bend that curves downward and then upward toward the middle. The bottom bend should be broad and rounded, with a centerline bend radius of approximately 35 mm.
3. Connect this to a middle vertical pipe rising upward. The middle vertical section should be approximately 110 mm tall.
4. At the top of the middle vertical pipe, create a smooth inverted U-shaped bridge that curves upward slightly and then bends down into a right vertical pipe. The top bridge should have a gentle rounded profile, with a span of approximately 50 mm and bend radius of about 25 mm.
5. Extend the right vertical pipe downward. This right vertical section should be approximately 115 mm tall.
6. At the bottom of the right vertical pipe, add a smooth 90-degree bend that turns into a long horizontal outlet pipe extending toward the right. The horizontal outlet section should be approximately 120 mm long.
7. The far right end of the horizontal pipe should remain open and clearly show the hollow interior.

The entire pipe must remain a single continuous hollow flow path. Keep all transitions smooth and rounded. Avoid sharp corners, disconnected parts, self-intersections, or blocked internal passages.

Both the top opening on the left side and the outlet opening on the far right side should be clearly visible.
