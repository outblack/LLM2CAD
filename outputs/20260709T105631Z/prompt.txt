Create a clean technical CAD model of an asymmetric four-port branched hollow pipe manifold.

The model must be one single continuous hollow tubular pipe system, not separate disconnected tubes.

The overall shape should resemble a branching pipe network viewed from a slightly tilted top-view or isometric perspective.

The pipe should have a mostly uniform circular outer diameter throughout the entire model. Keep the diameter consistent across the main pipe and all four branches.

The main passage should run from the left side toward the right side.

On the left side, the main pipe should split smoothly into two diagonal branches:
- one branch extending toward the upper-left direction,
- one branch extending toward the lower-left direction.

These two left branches should merge into the main central pipe using smooth Y-shaped blended transitions.

On the right side, the main pipe should split smoothly into two diagonal branches:
- one branch extending upward and to the right,
- one branch extending downward and to the right.

The right-side junction should also be a smooth Y-shaped pipe split with only minimal local thickening.

Do not create a bulbous spherical node, oval chamber, collector tank, bead-like swelling, or large rounded blob at any junction.

Junction areas may be slightly thicker only where pipes blend together, but they should remain close to the pipe diameter and should not look like separate balls or chambers.

All four branch ends must be open pipe ports:
- one open end at the upper-left,
- one open end at the lower-left,
- one open end at the upper-right,
- one open end at the lower-right.

Each open pipe end should clearly show hollow geometry, visible wall thickness, and rim edges.

Use smooth bends and continuous pipe surfaces. Avoid sharp corners, harsh Boolean seams, flat block-like intersections, disconnected parts, or inflated junction bulges.

The model should look mechanically plausible, like a fluid manifold, exhaust manifold, coolant pipe, or branching duct system.

Apply a clean CAD-style brushed metal or matte industrial metal material with a smooth semi-gloss finish.

The final model should appear as one connected hollow tubular manifold with four open ends, two branches on the left, two branches on the right, uniform pipe diameter, and smooth Y-shaped blended junctions without any ball-like or chamber-like swelling.
