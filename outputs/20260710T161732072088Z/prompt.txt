Create a single continuous hollow pipe with a complex three-dimensional curved shape.

The pipe should begin with a moderately long straight section and then transition smoothly into a broad upward bend, resembling a partial torus or a section of a large donut.

After the initial bend, continue the pipe along a rising spiral path. When viewed from above, this section should curve around a central region like a loose circular coil. When viewed from the side, the pipe should gradually rise rather than remaining on a flat plane.

Do not make the spiral perfectly uniform. Gradually reduce the turning radius as the pipe rises so that the lower portion forms a wider curve and the upper portion becomes slightly tighter.

After the rising spiral, guide the pipe into a three-dimensional S-shaped section. The pipe should first curve gently toward one side, then reverse direction and curve toward the opposite side while continuing to rise slightly. The bends must not look angular or segmented.

Following the S-shaped section, add another broad curved segment that rotates the pipe outward and slightly upward. This section should make the final outlet point away from the central spiral rather than toward it.

Use a constant circular hollow cross-section through most of the pipe. Near the final section, gradually reduce the pipe diameter using a smooth tapered transition. Do not create an abrupt change in diameter.

The pipe should have visible wall thickness and a clearly open internal passage. Both the starting end and final end should remain open, with the inner circular opening and outer rim clearly visible.

All sections must form one connected pipe body. The internal flow passage must remain continuous without blockage, self-intersection, pinching, or disconnected regions.

Maintain smooth tangent continuity between every section:
- straight section to broad upward bend,
- broad bend to rising spiral,
- spiral to three-dimensional S-curve,
- S-curve to outward bend,
- outward bend to tapered outlet.

Avoid sharp corners, sudden changes in direction, flat polygonal bends, or twisted cross-sections. The overall result should look like a deliberately designed freeform cooling pipe or fluid channel composed of smooth toroidal, helical, and spatial spline-like curves.
